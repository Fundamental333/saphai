About the Workshop

The rapid emergence of conversational AI, generative systems, and autonomous agents is reshaping how identities are constructed, inferred, and enacted across human-agent ecosystems. These systems increasingly collect, process, and act upon sensitive personal data, perform behavioral inference, integrate multimodal information, and mediate access to social and institutional resources.

HAIP (Human-Agent Identities & Privacy) positions privacy at the center of human-agent interaction. We examine agent-mediated data exposure, identity inference, cross-modal information leakage, secondary data use, and risks to historically understudied and structurally vulnerable populations. Beyond analyzing harms, HAIP also invites privacy-centered provotypes: early-stage, design-oriented explorations of how conversational agents can embed contextual integrity, data minimization, identity protection, and structural safeguards from the outset.

Call for Participation

We invite contributions that address identity, agency, and data governance in conversational and agentic systems. Submissions will be lightly reviewed by the organizers to ensure quality, relevance, and diversity of perspectives.

Submission Types

  • Short position papers (2–4 pages)
  • Privacy-centered provotypes (design-oriented, exploratory contributions)

Formats are intentionally lightweight to reduce barriers and welcome emerging work.

Suggested Themes

  • Agent-mediated data exposure and identity inference
  • Cross-modal information leakage (text, voice, vision, sensors)
  • Behavioral inference and sensitive attribute prediction
  • Secondary data use and data governance in agentic workflows
  • Privacy risks for structurally vulnerable or understudied populations
  • Contextual integrity, data minimization, identity protection by design

Accessibility & Inclusion

HAIP is committed to fostering an accessible, respectful, and inclusive environment. Workshop materials and communications will follow accessibility best practices, including accessible PDFs, alt text for images, and clear language.

  • Slides and presentations will follow accessibility guidelines (readable fonts, sufficient contrast).
  • Where feasible, we encourage live captioning and written summaries of breakout discussions.
  • Multiple participation modes: structured talks, moderated discussion, small-group breakouts, and written contributions.
  • The workshop adheres to ACM and SIGCHI Codes of Conduct with active facilitation to ensure equitable participation.
